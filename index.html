About Me

I am a graduate student at Claremont Graduate University specializing in Data Science and Anallytics. My work lies at the intersection of machine learning, ethical AI, and public policy — drawing from a background in development economics and international development. I care deeply about building systems that are inclusive, responsible, and driven by real-world needs.

During my graduate studies, I developed a keen interest in leveraging AI for equity and development. While diving deeper, I became extremely passionate in ethical use and deployment of AI. I started diving deeper into the domain of human-computer interaction specifically in the space of human centric AI feedback systems, i.e, Generative AI. I realised that while building GenAI applications such as chatbots have become the norm at most organisations, ethically deploying them is still a major challenge.
